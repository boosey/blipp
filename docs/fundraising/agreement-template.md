# Blipp Revenue Share Agreement

**IMPORTANT:** This is a plain-language draft of the agreement that will be signed between the founder of Blipp and each contributor. It is written to be readable without a law degree. **Before this agreement is signed by anyone, it must be reviewed and finalized by a licensed attorney** to make sure it complies with applicable securities and tax law in the relevant jurisdictions. Do not sign this version. Do not ask anyone else to sign this version. Use it as a starting point for a lawyer to clean up.

## 1. What this agreement is

The Contributor is giving the Founder a sum of money to help pay for the operating costs of Blipp. In return, the Contributor will receive a share of the money Blipp earns from its customers, on the terms described in this document.

This agreement is **not**:

- A loan
- A purchase of stock, shares, or any ownership interest in Blipp or any company related to Blipp
- A guarantee of any return of money
- A partnership or joint venture

The Contributor will not own any part of Blipp, will not have voting rights, will not participate in decisions about how Blipp is run, and will not be entitled to any proceeds from a sale of Blipp or any related company, except as specifically described below.

## 2. The contribution

The Contributor agrees to pay the Founder a one-time amount of **$[amount] USD** (the "Contribution") by wire transfer or ACH to the account specified by the Founder, within [7] days of signing this agreement.

The Contribution is non-refundable. Once paid, the Contributor cannot request its return for any reason.

The Founder agrees to use the Contribution solely to pay for operating costs of Blipp, including but not limited to: advertising, cloud infrastructure, software services, payment processing fees, and related business expenses. The Founder will not use the Contribution to pay personal expenses or a salary to themselves.

## 3. What the Contributor receives

In exchange for the Contribution, the Founder will pay the Contributor a percentage of Blipp's **Gross Revenue** (defined below) on the following terms:

### 3a. Primary revenue share

For every **$1,000 USD** of Contribution, the Contributor is entitled to receive **0.5%** of Blipp's Gross Revenue.

Example: A Contribution of $10,000 entitles the Contributor to 5.0% of Gross Revenue.

This primary revenue share continues until the Contributor has received **cumulative payments equal to 2.5 times** the original Contribution (the "Payback Cap").

Example: A Contribution of $10,000 has a Payback Cap of $25,000. Once the Contributor has received $25,000 in total payments under this agreement, the primary revenue share ends.

### 3b. Tail revenue share

After the Payback Cap is reached, the Contributor's revenue share drops to **0.1% of Gross Revenue for every $1,000 USD of Contribution**, for an additional **five (5) years** measured from the date the Payback Cap was reached. After five years, all payment obligations under this agreement end permanently.

Example: A Contribution of $10,000 earns a tail revenue share of 1.0% of Gross Revenue for 5 years after the Payback Cap of $25,000 is reached.

### 3c. Gross Revenue defined

"Gross Revenue" means all money actually received by the Founder (or any company formed by the Founder to operate Blipp) from Blipp customers for subscriptions, one-time purchases, or other use of the Product, **before** deducting any expenses, refunds, chargebacks, fees, or taxes — **except** that refunds and chargebacks actually paid out to customers may be subtracted from Gross Revenue for the month in which they occur.

Gross Revenue does **not** include:

- Money received from other contributors under similar agreements
- Money received from professional investors
- Money received from loans or credit lines
- Grants, awards, or prizes
- Proceeds from the sale of Blipp or the company that operates it
- Interest earned on bank accounts

## 4. How and when the Contributor gets paid

### 4a. Payment frequency

Payments under this agreement will be made **quarterly**, within 30 days after the end of each calendar quarter (that is, by April 30, July 31, October 31, and January 31 each year).

### 4b. Payment method

Payments will be made by ACH, wire transfer, or another method mutually agreed upon. The Contributor is responsible for keeping their banking information current with the Founder.

### 4c. Minimum payment threshold

If a given quarter's payment to the Contributor would be less than $25, it will be rolled over and combined with the following quarter's payment. This is to avoid wasting money on transfer fees for trivial amounts.

### 4d. Reports

With each quarterly payment, the Contributor will receive a report showing:

- Blipp's Gross Revenue for the quarter
- The percentage owed to the Contributor
- The amount being paid
- Cumulative amount paid to date under this agreement
- Amount remaining until the Payback Cap

In addition, the Contributor will receive the weekly and monthly status updates described in the prospectus, for as long as this agreement remains in effect.

## 5. What happens if Blipp is sold or transferred

### 5a. Sale of Blipp as an ongoing business

If the Founder sells, transfers, or merges Blipp (or the company that operates it) to another party, this agreement and all payment obligations under it will **transfer to the new owner**, who will become responsible for all future payments to the Contributor under the same terms. The Founder will ensure that any sale agreement includes this obligation.

### 5b. Shutdown of Blipp

If the Founder chooses to permanently shut down Blipp, all payment obligations under this agreement **end** on the date of shutdown. The Founder is not required to make any further payments. The Founder agrees to notify the Contributor in writing at least 30 days before a planned shutdown, except in cases of emergency or force majeure.

### 5c. Asset sale

If the Founder sells some or all of Blipp's assets (such as the brand name, customer list, or source code) to another party without transferring the ongoing business, the Contributor is entitled to receive the same revenue share percentage (up to the Payback Cap and including the tail) applied to the **net proceeds of the asset sale**, paid as a one-time payment within 60 days of the sale closing. This prevents a sale of assets from being used to avoid the revenue share.

## 6. The Founder's commitments

The Founder commits to:

- Using the Contribution only for Blipp's operating costs as described in Section 2
- Keeping honest books and records of Blipp's Gross Revenue and expenses
- Sending weekly status updates covering user counts, revenue, expenses, and progress, for as long as this agreement is in effect
- Sending quarterly payment reports as described in Section 4d
- Making all payments owed under this agreement on time
- Giving the Contributor at least 30 days' advance written notice before any sale, merger, asset transfer, or shutdown of Blipp
- Notifying the Contributor promptly of any material adverse event that could affect Blipp's ability to continue operating

## 7. The Contributor's acknowledgments

The Contributor acknowledges and agrees that:

- **The Contributor may lose the entire Contribution and receive nothing in return.** Early-stage software products fail more often than they succeed.
- The Contributor is making the Contribution with money they can afford to lose without financial hardship.
- The Founder has made no guarantees, promises, or projections of specific returns, revenue, user growth, or business outcomes.
- The Contributor has had the opportunity to ask questions and receive answers about Blipp and the terms of this agreement.
- The Contributor has been given the Blipp Friends & Family Funding Prospectus and has read it in full.
- The Contributor is not relying on any verbal statements or representations outside of this written agreement and the prospectus.
- The Contributor understands that this is not an equity investment and confers no ownership in Blipp.
- The Contributor is either an "accredited investor" or meets the applicable exemption requirements for small friends-and-family offerings in their jurisdiction, and will sign a separate statement confirming this if required.
- The Contributor understands that the Founder's personal relationship with the Contributor will not be affected by the outcome of this agreement, and that any disputes will be handled in good faith and with the preservation of the relationship as a top priority.

## 8. Taxes

Each party is responsible for their own taxes. The Founder will provide the Contributor with appropriate tax documentation (such as a 1099 form or equivalent) reporting payments made under this agreement each year, as required by applicable law. The Contributor should consult their own tax advisor about how to report these payments.

## 9. Dispute resolution

If a dispute arises under this agreement, the parties agree to:

1. **First, talk to each other in good faith.** Most disputes between people who care about each other can be resolved with a phone call.
2. **Second, try mediation.** If a direct conversation does not resolve the dispute, the parties will attempt mediation with a neutral third party before taking any other action.
3. **Only as a last resort, formal legal action.** Any formal legal proceedings will take place in [state/jurisdiction], under the laws of [state/jurisdiction].

The parties agree that the preservation of their personal relationship is more important than any individual dispute over money, and will approach any disagreement with that in mind.

## 10. General provisions

**Entire agreement.** This document, together with the Blipp Friends & Family Funding Prospectus, represents the entire agreement between the parties regarding the Contribution. No verbal agreements, side deals, or prior discussions are part of this agreement unless written here.

**Amendments.** This agreement can only be changed by a written document signed by both parties.

**Assignment.** The Contributor may not transfer their rights under this agreement to anyone else without the Founder's written consent, except to immediate family members in the event of death or incapacity.

**Severability.** If any part of this agreement is found to be unenforceable, the rest of the agreement remains in effect.

**No partnership.** Nothing in this agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, employment relationship, or agency relationship between the parties.

**Governing law.** This agreement is governed by the laws of [state/jurisdiction].
